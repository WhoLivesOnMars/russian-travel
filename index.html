Traveling in Russia

The real country isn’t on the news — it’s right here.

You have the upper bunk

We've seen it all there, haven’t we?

According to VCIOM polls, 95% of Russians dream of traveling somewhere, yet only 36% plan to spend their vacation in their own country. Many ask, “What’s left to see here at home?” In reality, Russia is an entire universe — from the gentle southern seas to the dense forests of the Sayan Mountains and the harsh ice-covered plains of the Putorana Plateau. And the best part? You can see all of this without millions in your bank account, a passport, or long-haul flights. Just like Vera Bashmakova — a courageous young mom who packed her three kids into a Lada and drove 20,000 kilometers across Russia. We’ve selected and described a few stunning destinations that are absolutely worthy of your vacation.

  • Curonian Spit

    URL

    park-kosa.ru

    Here, amidst forests and sand dunes, you can see two water horizons — the calm Curonian Lagoon on one side and the rippling waves of the Baltic Sea on the other. A unique natural area on the edge of the Russian enclave.

    But the Kaliningrad region doesn’t end there. Nearby awaits the westernmost point of Russia — the Baltic Spit — as well as the German heritage scattered across charming seaside towns. The atmosphere here leaves no room for rush, immersing you in the calm of nature and the scent of the cool, steely sea.

  • Kola Peninsula

    URL

    National Geographic

    Almost the entire peninsula lies beyond the Arctic Circle. The Saami tundra stretches here — with taiga to the south and the Arctic Ocean to the north, disguised as the Barents Sea.

    Maybe you’ve seen Zvyagintsev’s films or heard the story of the Arctic festival in Teriberka. Maybe the word “Khibiny” didn’t get buried under the snow of your school geography lessons. Maybe you’ve never looked into the Earth's crust-piercing superdeep borehole, and apatite only reminds you of apathy. But your dream of seeing the northern lights begins with a ticket to Murmansk.

  • Altai

    URL

    Facebook

    Altai is one of the most beautiful places in Russia — primarily because of its mountains. Drive along the ridge and you'll see pine-covered slopes, mountain rivers, and crystal-clear lakes. And if you roll down your car windows, you’ll discover the invisible wonder of this land — the mountain air.

    Altai has a moderate climate, making summer the best time to visit. That’s when you can fully experience the region’s rich flora and fauna. Moose roam the forests, eagles soar above the mountain ridges, and roe deer graze on the plains. Even the famous Pallas’s cats call Altai home.

  • Winter Baikal

    URL

    https://vk.com/baikalmile

    Everyone knows Baikal as the largest lake in the world. Many also know it as the biggest source of fresh water — and one of the most beautiful places in Russia.

    Of course, all of that is true. But Baikal is also the perfect place for skijoring competitions — a sport where a skier is harnessed to a motorcycle and the duo tries to reach maximum speed on ice. In March 2019, during the Baikal Mile festival, a world record was set: 197.011 km/h.

  • Karelia

    URL

    http://vodlozero.ru/

    Siberia doesn’t end at the Urals — it stretches all the way to Karelia. The Siberian larch, which defines the taiga, grows no farther west than Lake Vodlozero. But here, it reaches heights of 30 meters — the forests of Karelia’s national parks, untouched by axes due to their impassable swamps, have stood for centuries. Some of the pines are over 500 years old. You can touch a living being that saw the sun long before Ivan the Terrible did. In these virgin forests, you won’t find a single trail for hundreds of kilometers. And along the rare paths that do exist, trees are marked with bear claw scratches a couple of meters above the ground — just to remind you who’s in charge.
